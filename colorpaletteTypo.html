The theme we chose is marine-related; we chose this palette because it is the hue of water and sky.

For the sky, the blue light spreads more easily, and it comes to us from where the sun hits the atmosphere. Longer-wavelength visible light is easier for the oceans to get rid of, so the deeper they go, the darker blue the remaining light looks.

Typography

Garamond (EB Garamond) - Antique serif style

- Garamond is a 16th-century serif typeface designed by engraver Claude Garamond. Frequently used for body text and book printing. Letters with a reasonably organic structure that resembles penmanship but is slightly more rigid and upright.

- The characters are very expressive and they are used in bustling, playful contexts
