The theme we chose is marine-related; we chose this palette because it is the hue of water and sky.

For the sky, the blue light spreads more easily, and it comes to us from where the sun hits the atmosphere. Longer-wavelength visible light is easier for the oceans to get rid of, so the deeper they go, the darker blue the remaining light looks.

Example how we using color palette

The home page we use the #9ad2f5 color, it represents the sky and the color of the sea surface.

This color #16314b is use for background of the story page. It represents the bottom of the sea, the deeper you go, the darker the color of the sea

Typography

Garamond (EB Garamond) - Antique serif style

- Garamond is a 16th-century serif typeface designed by engraver Claude Garamond. Frequently used for body text and book printing. Letters with a reasonably organic structure that resembles penmanship but is slightly more rigid and upright.

- The characters are very expressive and they are used in bustling, playful contexts. For the title, Gramond will be utilised; according to research, this font type is also easy to read and does not create eye strain.

Source Serif (pro)

- It is a sans serif typeface designed specifically for usage in user interfaces.

We chose a san serif font because its design is simple, clean, and easy to read. It has thin handwriting and underlined lines, making it ideal for websites and web apps that require a lot of information sharing and transmission. Fonts assist readers in avoiding eye strain.
